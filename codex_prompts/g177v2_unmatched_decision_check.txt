## Quick design-check (under 250 words)

g177v2 just relaunched with `--allow-unmatched-donors`. Question: is this defensible vs cycle 55 adversarial?

## Reasoning chain

1. Cycle 55 attack demanded: "match Qwen3 on held-out C4 loss AND Frobenius distance, OR normalize lambda per donor." Disjunctive.
2. We chose the lambda-normalization branch. Empirically verified: `lambda_alt = lambda_qwen3 * sqrt(F²_qwen3 / F²_alt)` makes initial `||grad_anchor||` match exactly across donors.
3. Empirical g177v2 first launch: alt-donor 1234 NLL trajectory `{12.1@0, 8.0@100, 6.85@500, 6.57@1000, 6.46@1500}`. Log-extrap predicts ~5.3 at step 10000. Reaching 3.6 needs ~220k steps = ~15h, OOE on RTX 5090. Strict NLL matching is computationally infeasible on our hardware.
4. With `--allow-unmatched-donors`, alt donors save at NLL ~5.0-5.5 (whatever 10k steps produces). Main cells run anyway. Total wallclock ~3.5h.
