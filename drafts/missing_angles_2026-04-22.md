# Neural Genome — missing research angles (framing/measurement/claims)

1) **Your “untrained” null is too fake.** Shuffled / iid-Gaussian *surrogates of a trained activation cloud* are not the same thing as activations from a random-init network with the same architecture, LayerNorm/RMSNorm, and tokenization. If random-init (or partially-trained) models already sit near the same `sqrt(eff_rank)·α` curve, your “trained-specific invariant” becomes an architecture/normalization artifact and the whole story collapses.

2) **You’re calling it an invariant while the object isn’t uniquely defined.** `α` and `eff_rank` can swing with pre-vs-post norm, residual-stream vs sublayer outputs, token/position filtering, centering choice, and the tail-fit window; “mid-depth on C4” is a probe definition, not a law of nature. If the constant only holds in that sweet spot (and breaks in the last-7 layers where the “genome” allegedly lives), you’re mixing two different phenomena under one headline.

3) **Unconditional activation geometry is the wrong target for “capability.”** The atlas working only when the *entire stream is broken* is a smoking gun that unconditional means (and by extension unconditional spectra) mostly track a distribution prior, not context-conditional computation. If the invariant is computed on unconditional clouds, it may be measuring “how much the model matches its training marginals” rather than “how it represents conditional semantics.”

4) **You have no story for eigenvectors, only eigenvalues (numerology risk).** Tail-CDF universality and `eff_rank·α²≈18` say nothing about *which directions* carry what—yet your own null catalog shows “matching the spectrum” doesn’t transfer capability. If the universality lives in *orientation/alignment* (direction identity) rather than the singular values, then the scalar invariants are at best order parameters, not the substrate.

5) **The constant may be an optimizer/regime fingerprint, not a representation law.** Adam-style noise, weight decay, norm layers, and training curriculum can impose a stable noise+signal mixture (spiked-covariance style) that yields a repeatable spectrum without implying shared “semantics.” If instruction-tuning/RLHF/domain finetunes move the constant while capability stays, you must reframe the claim as “pretraining dynamics signature,” not “capability geometry.”

6) **You’re ignoring the functional object that theory usually cares about.** Activation covariance can look universal while the *input-output map* differs; the relevant spectra in IB/NTK/mean-field/edge-of-chaos arguments are Jacobians/Fisher/NTK kernels or dynamical isometry metrics. If your invariants don’t show up (or don’t correlate) at the sensitivity/kernel level, stop implying mechanistic meaning; if they do, the story becomes “criticality of training dynamics,” not “geometry of representations.”
